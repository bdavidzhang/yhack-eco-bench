## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Hackathon judges evaluating the ASUS sustainability track at YHack (Yale). Technical but time-constrained — they need to quickly grasp what was built and why it matters.

**Secondary**: ML researchers and engineers interested in sustainable inference — people who understand BPB, quantization, and power metrics. They want to compare configs and find efficient setups.

**Context**: Viewed on laptops and phones during a hackathon demo. First impressions matter. The Pareto frontier chart is the "hero moment" — it should land immediately.

### Brand Personality
**Solarpunk, warm, optimistic.**

Technology in harmony with nature. This isn't doom-and-gloom environmentalism — it's the belief that we can measure, optimize, and build AI systems that respect planetary limits. The data is rigorous, but the tone is hopeful.

### Emotional Goals
Target feelings: **impressed** and **excited**.
- **First 3 seconds**: "This is polished and serious" — credibility through craft, the team means business
- **First 30 seconds**: "Oh, they actually measured this" — the Pareto chart and SCI scores spark curiosity and excitement
- **After exploration**: "I want to show this to someone" — the regional calculator and leaderboards feel like discovery, not homework

### Aesthetic Direction
**Solarpunk data observatory** — organic warmth meets scientific precision.

- Warm, living greens (not neon, not corporate) — think sunlit leaves, not terminal text
- Light mode with warm neutral tints — never sterile white, never dark-mode-by-default
- Purple (from Kaizen) as structural depth — like twilight sky behind green canopy
- Data presented with confidence and breathing room — not crammed dashboards
- The Pareto frontier is the centerpiece — treated like a scientific figure, not a generic chart

**References (the feeling, not the layout)**:
- Climate TRACE (data credibility + mission warmth)
- Stripe Climate (polished data presentation)
- Our World in Data (clarity, authority, accessibility)

**Anti-references (what this is NOT)**:
- Generic anything — no templated layouts, no "could be any product" feel
- Dark mode neon monitoring tool
- Sterile corporate sustainability report with stock photos
- AI-generated landing page with glassmorphism and gradient text

**Typography direction**: Sharp sans-serif. Not soft/rounded — geometric or grotesque with crisp terminals. The Kaizen system uses Inter, but the display font (Tiempos Headline) is serif — consider replacing display usage with a distinctive sharp sans if it better serves the solarpunk-technical tone.

### Design Principles

1. **Data is the design** — The numbers, charts, and comparisons ARE the visual interest. Don't decorate around them; make them beautiful directly.

2. **Warm rigor** — Scientific credibility presented with human warmth. ISO standards and solarpunk aren't contradictions — they're the whole point.

3. **Green earns its meaning** — Green means "sustainable" and "good" here. Use it purposefully for SCI scores, Pareto indicators, and efficiency wins. Never decoratively.

4. **Progressive depth** — The dashboard should be instantly scannable, the leaderboards reward exploration, and the methodology page rewards careful reading. Each layer adds detail.

5. **Optimism through evidence** — The site's core message is hopeful: you CAN run LLMs efficiently. The data proves it. Let the Pareto frontier tell that story.

### Constraints
- Must use Kaizen Design System CSS tokens (no custom color values outside the token system)
- Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS (no framework)
- Chart.js for visualizations
- WCAG AA minimum, with `prefers-reduced-motion` support
- Must work on mobile (hackathon judges on phones)
- Static site deployable to Vercel/Netlify/GitHub Pages
